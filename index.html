What is SCDS?

SCDS ratings roughly describe how much synthesis was used to make a piece of content, particularly regarding generative AI usage.

The score does not describe the quality of a product.

Read the scoring method →

Why it matters

Clients of any product online want to know if AI was used in the creation process, and more importantly, what AI was used for. A SCDS rating is an easy-to-get disclosure for your material that satisfies that need in an easy-to-understand way.

Rating scale

Ratings describe how frequently AI was used, its role, and whether it impacts the user experience.

AAA

No AI usage.

OIB

Occasional implementation, not impacting experience.

OIE

Occasional implementation, impacting experience.

OPB

Occasional planning, not impacting experience.

OPE

Occasional planning, impacting experience.

OEB

Occasional implementation and planning, not impacting experience.

OEE

Occasional implementation and planning, impacting experience.

FIB

Frequent implementation, not impacting experience.

FIE

Frequent implementation, impacting experience.

FPB

Frequent planning, not impacting experience.

FPE

Frequent planning, impacting experience.

FEB

Frequent implementation and planning, not impacting experience.

FEE

Frequent implementation and planning, impacting experience.

How ratings work

The questionnaire directly determines a three-letter rating. The first letter describes the frequency of AI usage: A (absent), O (occasional), or F (frequent). The second describes AI’s role: A (absent), I (implementation), P (planning), or E (both). The third describes scope: A (absent), B (backend), or E (experience).

An AAA rating means no AI was used anywhere in development. If AI was used, the remaining answers combine to create one of the twelve O or F ratings. This tool is a disclosure aid, not a certification.
